Parmesan was a simple cheese. He didn’t need much, only his best friend: Gorgonzola. The pair were inseparable, anyone could tell. Sometimes they would be teased for being romantically involved, but they always were best friends… Right? Well, there was something Parmesan didn’t know. Yes, Gorgonzola adored Parmesan, but Parmesan was the same cheese who filled his head at night while he tried to rest. It made Gorgonzola feel guilty, guilty for liking his best friend out of any cheese in Cheddartopia.

Sometimes, he wished Parmesan would look at him the same way Gorgonzola looked at him. But why should Parmesan love him? Plus, it was a known fact that Parmesan was a… “ladies cheese” If you get the gist. Still, nothing would cease Gorgonzola’s agonizing yearning for his best friend. This is why it hurt dearly when Parmesan introduced his girlfriend, Camembert, to Gorgonzola. When he got home that night, he sobbed into his pillow until morning broke.

For days he found himself unable to face Parmesan. Nothing felt right, and Gorgonzola ended up in a serious depressive state. One day, while moping around at home, he received a call from Parmesan. The idea of talking to Parm was terrifying to Gorgonzola, but he found himself answering the phone anyways. For a moment, neither of them spoke.

“Zola… Why won’t you answer my texts? I’ve been trying to talk to you for days!” Parmesan’s voice was full of concern, and Gorgonzola could perfectly picture him biting his lip nervously. “Are you okay? Are you sick? I’m just worried.”

“I-I’m fine. A-okay over here!” Gorgonzola was always a bad liar, a fact that Parm knew well. He wiped a hand over his cheesy face. He was so scared of his voice cracking, of showing his best friend his true feelings. “I’m just super busy.”

“Zola… I know that’s not true. I’m coming over, okay?”

“NO!” Gorgonzola accidentally shouted. “No, you should stay with Camembert. I don’t think she would love it if you came to see me.” Why? Gorgonzola just had a bad feeling that Parmesan’s girlfriend really disliked him.

“That’s fair. She seems to always want me around, I can’t escape!” Parmesan chuckled, but it sounded forced. “Actually, she’s starting to freak me out with this behavior. Oh, she’s calling me, I gotta go.”

“Okay… bye.” Gorgonzola could barely speak, he missed Parmesan so much. For another moment they both fell silent again. It felt awkward between them, something new. Before Camembert, they could never describe their bond as awkward. Everything was changing, and Gorgonzola hated it.

“Look out for yourself, Zola. And I’m always here.” And then Parmesan was gone, and Gorgonzola was left staring at his living room wall, holding back the buckets of fondue tears.

Parmesan wished to see Gorgonzola. He missed his best friend so much, it felt like some other cheese turned him swiss. Life with Camembert was… new to him. Sure, she was sweet, but it always felt too much and too forced. If he had to, he’d explain her as a poisonous flower with sweet nectar.

“Honeyboo!” That meant Camembert was home. Parmesan was standing outside her front door, hoping she would be gone, but the odds were never in his favor. The door swung open, revealing Camembert with her flower on her cheesy head. “Come in!” Her smile was way too big.

Once inside, Parmesan noticed a weird box stuffed in the corner of the living room. It didn’t raise any warning bells in his head though, he thought nothing of it, which was a major mistake. He was too distracted. He was thinking about Gorgonzola, like always.

“Here! Have some milk!” Camembert grinned widely, shoving a glass of milk into Parmesan’s hands. She didn’t seem like no was an answer, so he quickly gulped it down. The entire time, she was staring at him with her little beady eyes. Wiping his cheesy mouth with a cheesy sleeve, he leaned back into the couch. It all felt way too uncomfortable.

Suddenly, Parmesan began to feel woozy. Standing, he muttered something about having to go to the bathroom. He took one step… two steps… three steps before he fell flat on his face. His last thought before passing out was that he had to see Gorgonzola again.

After crying for much too long, Gorgonzola wiped his eyes free from tears. He decided he was done crying over the situation, and would call Parmesan. He couldn’t give a cheese curd what Camembert thought of him. He needed to see his best friend again.

The phone rang too long. Gorgonzola almost figured that Parmesan was too busy doing Grilled Cheese knows what with his girlfriend, when someone picked up. Sitting up straighter, he opened his mouth to speak, when someone else spoke instead.

“Hi, Gorgonzola. I have Parmesan! And I will have him forever and ever!” And then Camembert cackled before hanging up. Prickles stuck up across Gorgonzola’s cheese body. She was evil! And he had to stop her, and save Parmesan. He had to. He needed a plan that would work. And maybe, if it actually did work out, he could tell Parm how he really feels.

At Camembert’s house, Parmesan sat in the box in the corner. When revealed, it turned out to be a cage of cold and malicious metal bars. He was terrified as Camembert paced around in front of him. She seemed to be smiling to herself about something, and it made his terror worse. Crawling to the front of the cage, he clung to the bars in desperation.

“Why, Camembert, why?!” Parmesan cried in shock, still thinking about Gorgonzola. Oh how he missed his Zola. She turned sharply as sharp cheddar to face Parmesan, and giggled in a sickly sweet tone.

“Because, Parmey! It’s so very FUN.” With that, she thwacked his cheese hands away from the bars, and he scrambled backwards in fear, cowering at the back of the cage. Parmesan would give anything to have Zola here. Absolutely anything.

WHAT IS GORGONZOLA'S PLAN?!

At Camembert’s home, Parmesan tried yet again to scooch forwards to the front of the cage. Noticing this, Camembert raised her cheesy hand to smack him away, when a rumbling came from above. Looking up in confusion, she realized much too late what was happening. Crashing through the roof, a massive grand piano fell straight onto Camembert, squishing her into cream cheese almost instantly. From the hole in the roof that the musical instrument, a rope fell down. Sliding down the rope was Gorgonzola. Easily breaking the lock of the cage, he helped Parmesan to his feet. “Parm I need you to know something!” He started, but Parmesan cut him off. “No… I love you, Zola. I’ve been in love with you for as long as we’ve known one another. What were you going to say?” Parmesan took a speechless Gorgonzola’s hands. “I-I love you too!” And then they shared a passionate kiss that tasted like a fancy cheese platter.
At Camembert’s home, Parmesan tried yet again to scooch forwards to the front of the cage. Noticing this, Camembert raised her cheesy hand to smack him away, when a high pitch shrieking came from outside the window. Looking through the glass in confusion, she realized much too late what was happening. Gorgonzola came flying though, breaking the window instantly. Tumbling into Camembert, he used his new found fire powers to melt her into fondue. Once she seeped into the floorboards, which took almost no time, Gorgonzola broke the lock of the cage easily, flying Parmesan to safety. Back in the city center of Cheddartopia they finally got to speak to one another. “Parm I need you to know something!” He started, but Parmesan cut him off. “No… I love you, Zola. I’ve been in love with you for as long as we’ve known one another. What were you going to say?” Parmesan took a speechless Gorgonzola’s hands. “I-I love you too!” And then they shared a passionate kiss that tasted like a fancy cheese platter.
At Camembert’s home, Parmesan tried yet again to scooch forwards to the front of the cage. Noticing this, Camembert raised her cheesy hand to smack him away, when a loud knock at the door echoed through the house. Growling at Parmesan to stay still, she went to answer the door, plastering her villainous behavior with a sweet smile. Opening the door, Camembert found herself face to face with… “Gorgonzola.” She said with disdain, looking him up and down in slow and intimidating disgust. “Please free Parmesan! He did nothing!” Gorgonzola begged. He clasped his cheesy hands together, breaking out the classic puppy eyes with a pouting bottom lip. It had to work, this trick always worked for him. She didn’t speak for a moment, so he stayed like that, in that begging mood. Then, she laughed at him like he was an idiot, and slammed the door in his face. The force of the door caused Gorgonzola to fall backwards onto the sidewalk. It was over. He would never see Parmesan ever again, never tell him how he feels! Laying, staring at the too perfect sky, he began to cry for real this time, afraid he would never stop.
In Cheddartopia, humans were the biggest threat of all. They would eat any cheese in sight, devour them whole without a second thought. They were absolute nightmare fuel. Unfortunately, Gorgonzola was in too much of a rush to remember how to protect himself from the threats of the human world. Running through the streets, he didn’t see where he was going, until he ran directly into a big… rock? In the middle of the road? Confusion washed over Gorgonzola, almost as fast as the giant shadow did. It, in fact, was not a rock. Instead it was a human. A really scary, cheese eating, slobbering human. Gorgonzola began to run, but the human scooped him up with ease in its big, lumpy, ugly hand. He struggled helplessly against the grip of the human as he was lifted into the air. As his inevitable, drooly and digestive death loomed over him, Gorgonzola screamed his last words: “I LOVE YOU PARM!” And then he was gone, and far away, in his cage at Camembert’s house, Parm could hear the faint last words of his only true love.
So… Gorgonzola was going to execute his plan, but he got in his head. His thoughts whirled around, telling him the plan wasn’t detailed enough. In the end, he listened. For centuries, Gorgonzola worked and worked. He worked through raging war and apocalyptic climate change. By the time he finished, Cheddartopia was no more. He lived in a dystopian society of MURICA. MURICA were colonizing beings, evil and cruel, and they despised cheese. When he realized his dilemma, Gorgonzola could cry. He didn’t know if Parmesan was even alive now! He wasn’t sure he himself was alive. He was an ancient crusty cheese now, one of the oldest to still be around. His age didn’t help him either! He couldn’t complete the plan in his state, he was simply too elderly! Gorgonzola died the day he finished the plan. He died of heartbreak, knowing he would never have been able to save his love.
Through many tests and equations of Gorgonzola going through any possible ways to save Parmesan, he found something. A way to travel through realities in portals. He could use it to get to Parm! Open a portal in the house, take out Camembert quick and easy, then escape with Parmesan through the portal. Easy! So, he did that! He ripped over an inter-reality portal, slipping through. Once in Camembert’s house, he used magic he gained while traveling through his portal. Breaking open the cage in which held Parmesan, they stood in front of the portal, hand in cheesy hand. “Ready?” Gorgonzola asked, turning to face Parmesan. “I’ll always be ready as long as it’s you.” The smile that Parmesan gave Gorgonzola said everything, every yearning emotion of love, every ounce of longing, it showed it all. Squeezing each other’s hands, they stepped through. On the other side, Gorgonzola exploded in a coughing fit. After the hacking died away, he realized something… or should we say she? Looking at Parmesan, then HERself, she realized they’d entered the wrong reality! They were female cheeses! “P-Parm! I’m so sorry, I don’t know how to get back–” Gorgonzola began to panic, hands shaking from anxiety, but Parmesan cut her off. “It’s fine, Gorg. I think I like this reality. What if we… stayed?” Parmesan suggested, holding onto Gorgonzola’s shoulders. Taking a deep breath, she nodded. Yeah. They would stay here, and it would be absolutely magical. “I love you, Parm.” She sighed, leaning into Parmesan. “I love you too.” She told Gorgonzola, kissing her sweet and slow.
It took Gorgonzola weeks to form his plan. In the end, he felt stupid for taking so long for such a simple plan. Break down the door, kick Camembert to misery, and rescue Parmesan. Easy peasy cheesy squeezy! Arriving at her house, Gorgonzola did just that. He busted down the front door, leaping at Camembert with fury. As he was close to defeating her, she began to laugh. The reaction caught him off guard, why was she laughing? “It’s too late! He’s already tortured enough.” She grinned wickedly before Gorgonzola sent her to be punished by the Grilled Cheese. Turning to the cage, he saw the back of it was cast in shadows. In the shadows sat Parmesan, legs pulled close to his chest, rocking back and forth. “Parm? It’s me, Zola! I’m going to open the cage, okay?” When Parmesan didn’t answer, Gorgonzola knew something was definitely wrong. And yet he broke off the lock, swinging the cage door wide open. For a moment, neither of them moved… until Parmesan launched from the shadows, tackling Gorgonzola with full force. Pinned to the floor, Gorgonzola felt something sharp sink into his side. “PARM! NO! NO!” Parmesan was eating him alive. As he was eaten away, bit by cheesy bit, his last words were swirled with a drabble of cheese dip blood. “But, Parm… I love you.”
